Surveillance Assessment Report: TechSecure Solutions

Date: November 6, 2024

Auditor: Jane Smith, Lead Auditor

Scope: IT systems, physical security, data access, and user training for ISO 27001 compliance.

1. Introduction

Purpose of the Report: This surveillance assessment report aims to verify that TechSecure Solutions continues to meet ISO 27001 standards for information security management.

Scope of the Assessment: The audit focuses on information security policies, access control, incident response, and employee training within TechSecure Solutions' main office and its cloud infrastructure.

2. Summary of Previous Compliance Status

In the previous assessment (March 2024), TechSecure Solutions was found fully compliant with most ISO 27001 controls, with minor issues in user access management and data backup processes. The company implemented corrective actions, including enhanced logging and backup improvements.

3. Assessment Methodology

Assessment Frequency: Quarterly surveillance assessment.

Approach: The assessment included document reviews, technical control testing, employee interviews, and a physical security walkthrough.

Standards Referenced: ISO 27001:2013 Information Security Management System.

4. Control Review

Access Control

Objective: Ensure that only authorized personnel have access to sensitive data and systems.

Findings: Review of access logs and role-based permissions revealed that terminated employeesâ€™ access was not always promptly revoked.

Compliance Status: Partially Compliant

Evidence: HR logs showed a delay in deactivating user accounts for three out of 20 terminated employees.

Risk Assessment and Management

Objective: Continually identify and mitigate security risks.

Findings: The risk register is updated regularly, but new risks associated with remote work have not been assessed since July 2023.

Compliance Status: Non-Compliant

Evidence: Risk assessment documentation lacks updates on remote access vulnerabilities, despite the increase in remote work.

5. Observations and Non-Conformities

  • Access Control Issue: Delays in revoking access for terminated employees present potential unauthorized access risks.
  • Risk Assessment Gaps: Lack of updated risk assessment for remote work.
  • Data Encryption Protocols: Outdated protocols on a legacy server could lead to data vulnerabilities.
  • Incident Response: Lack of incident response updates post-phishing attack.
  • Training Gaps: Three employees missed mandatory security training timelines.

6. Recommendations for Improvement

  • Implement automated deactivation processes to ensure that terminated employees lose access promptly.
  • Review and update the risk register to address new risks associated with remote work.
  • Upgrade legacy server protocols to TLS 1.2 or higher for enhanced data security.
  • Revise the incident response plan based on lessons learned from the recent phishing attack.
  • Establish a tracking system to ensure all employees complete security training within 30 days of hire.

7. Summary of Findings and Overall Compliance Status

TechSecure Solutions demonstrates a strong commitment to security, with compliance in many areas. However, several non-conformities and minor issues require attention to ensure continued compliance with ISO 27001 standards. Overall, the organization is partially compliant and must address identified issues by the next audit.
