Gap Analysis Table: CMMC Level 3 Compliance for TechDefense Solutions

CMMC Domain Current Gap Desired State (Goal) Actions to Bridge the Gap
Access Control (AC) Decentralized access policies with limited MFA, leading to inconsistent security for CUI access. Standardized access control policies across all departments, with MFA and automated tracking for all CUI systems. Implement MFA company-wide, establish role-based access, and use automated tracking to ensure consistent access control.
Incident Response (IR) No formal, unified incident response plan for CUI, leading to reactive responses across different teams. Documented, proactive incident response plan specific to CUI, with trained personnel and defined response protocols. Develop a CUI-focused incident response plan, conduct regular training and drills, and deploy real-time CUI monitoring.
Audit and Accountability (AU) Incomplete logging across departments, with no centralized system for tracking access or auditing CUI activity. Centralized logging system with scheduled audits to monitor all CUI access and ensure accountability. Implement a centralized SIEM system, establish audit schedules, and define log retention policies.
System Protection (SC) Limited network segmentation and varied encryption standards, creating vulnerabilities in CUI data handling. Full network segmentation and end-to-end encryption to protect all CUI data flows across departments. Segment networks handling CUI and apply end-to-end encryption to data flows.
Personnel Security (PS) Inconsistent background checks and lack of standardized CUI security training for personnel handling sensitive data. Consistent vetting processes and CMMC-specific training for all personnel accessing CUI, with training tracked. Conduct background checks, provide CMMC-specific training, and track completion rates.
Risk Management (RM) Ad hoc risk assessments without a unified approach, making it hard to identify and mitigate CUI-specific risks. Structured risk assessment framework with a POA&M to systematically address vulnerabilities in CUI protection. Regular risk assessments, focused on CUI, and a formal POA&M to prioritize and address identified risks.
Maintenance (MA) Untracked and unscheduled maintenance for CUI systems, leaving systems vulnerable. Scheduled, documented maintenance to ensure CUI systems are secure and regularly updated. Create a maintenance schedule for CUI systems, document all updates, and track compliance post-maintenance.
Configuration Management (CM) No documented configurations or change approval process for CUI systems, risking unauthorized changes. Baseline configurations and a formal change management process to maintain secure system settings. Establish baseline configurations, enforce a change approval process, and automate configuration tracking.
Physical Security (PE) Inconsistent physical access control and visitor tracking for CUI storage areas, risking unauthorized access. Secure and controlled physical access to CUI storage locations, with regular monitoring and visitor logs. Install keycard access systems, maintain visitor logs, and conduct quarterly physical audits of CUI storage areas.
